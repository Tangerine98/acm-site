ACM India Annual Event 2019

ACM India Annual Event 2019

The ACM India Annual Event 2019 was conducted at Rajagiri School of Engineering and Technology on the 8th of February 2019. ACM India has been organizing annual flagship events to discuss trends in science and technology, and to celebrate ACM's spirit and India's accomplishments in computing. This event was organized by ACM Cochin Professional Chapter in association with the Rajagiri School of Engineering & Technology, Cochin, Kerala.

The keynote speakers were Prof. Sanjeev Arora from Princeton University, USA who spoke on the topic "What is Machine Learning and Deep Learning?", Prof. Sanghamitra Bandyopadhyay from Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata who talked on "Multiobjective Clustering and Applications", Prof. Charles E. Leiserson from Massachusets Institute of Technology, USA who spoke on "The Resurgence of Software Performance Engineering" and Prof. Noam Nisan from Hebrew University of Jerusalem whose talk was on "The Complexity of Pricing".

Tech-Talk on Machine Learning

Tech-Talk on Machine Learning

Machine learning (ML) is the scientific study of algorithms and statistical models that computer systems use to progressively improve their performance on a specific task. Machine learning algorithms build a mathematical model of sample data, known as "training data", in order to make predictions or decisions without being explicitly programmed to perform the task.To update the young minds with new technologies, the ACM FISAT hosted a tech talk by Dr Shihabudheen K.V who is working as an Assistant professor, Department of Electrical &Electronics, TKM College of Engineering, Kollam. The talk was held on September 11, 2018 at the conference hall from 10 am to 12.00 pm. The tech talk was inaugurated by the Computer Science and Engineering HOD. The class dealt with introduction to machine learning, it's applications and various algorithms. Indeed it was a worth session for all.

ACM Student Chapter FISAT Inauguration

ACM Student Chapter FISAT Inauguration

ACM, the world's largest educational and scientific computing society, delivers resources that advance computing as a science and a profession. ACM’s Professional and Student chapters worldwide serve as hubs of activity for ACM members and the computing community at large. They provide seminars, lectures, learning forums and networking opportunities with peers and experts across the computing spectrum. An ACM Student chapter was started by Department of Computer Science & Engineering, at FISAT and was inaugurated by Dr. G Santhosh Kumar, Associate Professor, CUSAT on 22nd June 2016. It aims to serve as a gateway to forums, panel discussions, and symposia that further aids student's professional development.
